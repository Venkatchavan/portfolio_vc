Collaborative Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning Thesis

Advanced Research in Multi-Agent Systems and Deep Reinforcement Learning

Project Overview

This thesis explores the frontier of Collaborative Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (CMARL), investigating how multiple intelligent agents can work together to solve complex problems that are beyond the capabilities of single-agent systems.

Technologies Used

  • Python & TensorFlow
  • OpenAI Gym
  • Multi-Agent Particle Environment
  • Deep Q-Networks (DQN)
  • Actor-Critic Methods

Key Features

  • Multi-agent coordination algorithms
  • Distributed learning mechanisms
  • Communication protocols between agents
  • Performance evaluation metrics
  • Scalability analysis

Research Contributions

  • Novel coordination strategies
  • Improved learning efficiency
  • Scalable architecture design
  • Comprehensive performance analysis
  • Future research directions

Applications

  • Autonomous vehicle coordination
  • Robotic swarm intelligence
  • Resource allocation systems
  • Smart grid management
  • Game theory applications

Results & Impact

The research demonstrated significant improvements in learning efficiency and coordination effectiveness compared to traditional single-agent approaches. The proposed algorithms showed promising results in complex multi-agent environments with varying degrees of cooperation and competition.
