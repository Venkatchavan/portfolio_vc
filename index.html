About Me

Innovator. Developer. Dreamer.

I am a Master's student in Big Data and Artificial Intelligence at SRH Berlin University, specializing in data engineering, machine learning, and large language models (LLMs). My professional experience spans data engineering at Ford, data science at AI Variant, and software testing at Siemens, giving me a strong foundation in both industry-grade engineering and applied AI.

My current research focuses on Coordinated Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (CMARL) for autonomous driving, where I design and test intelligent systems capable of decentralized decision-making in mixed-traffic environments. Beyond my thesis, I have explored NLP applications, including sentiment analysis of religious texts and chatbot development with Mistral 7B and LangChain.

Passionate about building interpretable and impactful AI solutions, I aim to bridge the gap between cutting-edge research and real-world deployment, particularly in healthcare, autonomous systems, and data-driven decision-making.
